## Getting Started

### Creating Your Account

1. **Visit the Registration Page**
   - Go to the Fact Checker website
   - Click "Sign Up" or "Register"

2. **Fill in Your Information**
   - Enter a valid email address
   - Create a strong password (minimum 8 characters)
   - Confirm your password
   - Click "Create Account"

3. **Email Verification**
   - Check your email for a verification message
   - Click the verification link to activate your account

### Logging In

1. **Access the Login Page**
   - Click "Log In" on the homepage
   - Enter your email and password
   - Click "Sign In"

2. **Stay Logged In**
   - Check "Remember Me" to stay logged in longer
   - Your session will remain active for security

### First Steps After Registration

1. **Complete Your Profile**
   - Add a profile picture (optional)
   - Write a brief bio about yourself
   - Set your notification preferences

2. **Explore the Platform**
   - Browse recent facts on the homepage
   - Try searching for topics that interest you
   - Read community guidelines

## Submitting Facts

### How to Submit a Fact

1. **Click "Submit Fact"**
   - Available on the homepage and navigation menu
   - Must be logged in to submit

2. **Write Your Fact**
   - Enter your fact in the content box
   - Minimum 10 characters, maximum 2000 characters
   - Be clear, concise, and accurate

3. **Add Supporting Resources**
   - Include links to credible sources
   - Add images if relevant (optional)
   - Provide context or additional information

4. **Review and Submit**
   - Double-check your fact for accuracy
   - Ensure sources are credible
   - Click "Submit Fact"

### Best Practices for Fact Submission

#### Content Guidelines
- **Be Accurate**: Only submit facts you can verify
- **Cite Sources**: Always include credible references
- **Be Original**: Don't duplicate existing facts
- **Stay Objective**: Present information neutrally

#### Source Requirements
- **Credible Sources**: Use reputable websites, academic papers, news outlets
- **Multiple Sources**: Include several sources when possible
- **Recent Sources**: Use up-to-date information
- **Accessible Links**: Ensure links work and are publicly accessible

#### Writing Style
- **Clear Language**: Write in simple, understandable terms
- **Proper Grammar**: Use correct spelling and punctuation
- **Neutral Tone**: Avoid biased or emotional language
- **Complete Information**: Include all necessary context

### Editing Your Facts

#### When You Can Edit
- Within 24 hours of submission
- Before receiving significant community votes
- If no active discussions are ongoing

#### How to Edit
1. Go to your profile and find the fact
2. Click "Edit" next to the fact
3. Make your changes
4. Add a note explaining the edit
5. Click "Save Changes"

#### Edit History
- All edits are tracked and visible
- Users can see what changed and when
- Original version is preserved

## Voting System

### Understanding Votes

#### Fact Verification
- **Fact**: Vote if you believe the statement is true
- **Fake**: Vote if you believe the statement is false
- **Neutral**: Don't vote if you're unsure

#### Comment Voting
- **Upvote**: For helpful, relevant, or insightful comments
- **Downvote**: For unhelpful, irrelevant, or inappropriate comments

### How to Vote

1. **Read Carefully**: Thoroughly read the fact and any sources
2. **Research**: Verify information from multiple sources
3. **Vote Responsibly**: Only vote if you're confident
4. **Change Your Vote**: You can update your vote if you learn new information

### Vote Impact

#### Community Verification
- Facts with more "Fact" votes are considered more reliable
- Facts with more "Fake" votes may be flagged for review
- Balanced voting indicates controversial or complex topics

#### User Reputation
- Consistent accurate voting builds your reputation
- Your voting history affects your credibility score
- High-reputation users' votes carry more weight

### Voting Ethics

#### Do's
- Research before voting
- Vote based on evidence, not opinion
- Consider multiple perspectives
- Update votes when presented with new evidence

#### Don'ts
- Don't vote on your own content
- Don't vote based on personal bias
- Don't coordinate voting with others
- Don't vote without reading the content

## Reporting Content

### When to Report

#### Inappropriate Content
- **Misinformation**: Deliberately false information
- **Spam**: Repetitive or promotional content
- **Harassment**: Personal attacks or bullying
- **Hate Speech**: Discriminatory or offensive language
- **Copyright Violation**: Unauthorized use of copyrighted material

#### Suspicious Activity
- **Fake Accounts**: Accounts that appear to be bots or impersonators
- **Vote Manipulation**: Coordinated voting or gaming the system
- **Multiple Accounts**: Users operating multiple accounts

### How to Report

1. **Find the Report Button**
   - Available on facts, comments, and user profiles
   - Usually represented by a flag icon

2. **Select Report Reason**
   - Choose the most appropriate category
   - Be specific about the violation

3. **Provide Details**
   - Explain why you're reporting the content
   - Include any relevant context
   - Add evidence if available

4. **Submit Report**
   - Click "Submit Report"
   - You'll receive a confirmation

### After Reporting

#### What Happens Next
- Reports are reviewed by moderators
- Action is taken based on community guidelines
- You may receive updates on the report status

#### Report Status
- **Pending**: Under review by moderators
- **Resolved**: Action has been taken
- **Dismissed**: No violation found

## Community Guidelines

### Core Principles

#### Accuracy First
- Only share information you can verify
- Include credible sources for all facts
- Correct mistakes when you discover them
- Don't spread misinformation

#### Respectful Discourse
- Treat all community members with respect
- Engage in civil, constructive discussions
- Avoid personal attacks or harassment
- Respect different viewpoints and perspectives

#### Quality Content
- Submit original, valuable content
- Avoid spam or repetitive posts
- Use proper grammar and spelling
- Make meaningful contributions to discussions

### Prohibited Content

#### Misinformation
- Deliberately false or misleading information
- Conspiracy theories without credible evidence
- Medical misinformation that could cause harm
- False information about public figures or events

#### Harmful Content
- Hate speech or discriminatory language
- Harassment, bullying, or personal attacks
- Threats of violence or harm
- Content that promotes illegal activities

#### Spam and Manipulation
- Repetitive or promotional content
- Vote manipulation or coordinated inauthentic behavior
- Multiple accounts operated by the same person
- Commercial spam or advertising

### Consequences of Violations

#### Warning System
- **First Violation**: Warning and education
- **Second Violation**: Temporary restrictions
- **Third Violation**: Account suspension
- **Severe Violations**: Immediate account termination

#### Types of Restrictions
- **Comment Restrictions**: Limited ability to comment
- **Submission Restrictions**: Cannot submit new facts
- **Voting Restrictions**: Limited voting privileges
- **Account Suspension**: Temporary account deactivation
- **Account Termination**: Permanent account removal

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

#### Login Problems
**Problem**: Can't log in to your account
**Solutions**:
- Check your email and password are correct
- Try resetting your password
- Clear your browser cache and cookies
- Disable browser extensions temporarily
- Try a different browser or device

#### Password Reset
**Problem**: Forgot your password
**Solutions**:
1. Click "Forgot Password" on the login page
2. Enter your email address
3. Check your email for reset instructions
4. Follow the link to create a new password
5. Contact support if you don't receive the email

#### Notification Issues
**Problem**: Not receiving notifications
**Solutions**:
- Check your notification settings
- Verify your email address is correct
- Check your spam/junk folder
- Ensure notifications aren't blocked by your email provider
- Update your email preferences

#### Voting Problems
**Problem**: Can't vote on facts or comments
**Solutions**:
- Make sure you're logged in
- Check if you've already voted on the content
- Verify your account isn't restricted
- Try refreshing the page
- Clear your browser cache

#### Content Not Loading
**Problem**: Facts or comments not displaying
**Solutions**:
- Refresh the page
- Check your internet connection
- Try a different browser
- Disable ad blockers temporarily
- Clear browser cache and cookies

## FAQ

### Account and Registration

**Q: Is registration required to use Fact Checker?**
A: You can browse facts without an account, but you need to register to submit facts, vote, comment, or receive notifications.

**Q: Can I change my email address?**
A: Yes, you can update your email address in Account Settings. You'll need to verify the new email address.

**Q: How do I delete my account?**
A: Contact support to request account deletion. Note that your public contributions (facts and comments) may remain visible but will be anonymized.

**Q: Can I have multiple accounts?**
A: No, each person is allowed only one account. Multiple accounts may result in all accounts being suspended.

### Facts and Content

**Q: How long does it take for a fact to be approved?**
A: Facts are published immediately but may be reviewed by moderators. Most reviews are completed within 24 hours.

**Q: Can I edit a fact after submitting it?**
A: Yes, you can edit facts within 24 hours of submission, provided they haven't received significant community engagement.

**Q: What makes a good source?**
A: Good sources are credible, authoritative, recent, and publicly accessible. Examples include academic papers, reputable news outlets, government websites, and established organizations.

**Q: Can I submit the same fact that someone else posted?**
A: No, duplicate facts are not allowed. Search first to see if the fact already exists. You can add additional sources or context to existing facts through comments.

### Voting and Reputation

**Q: How is the reputation score calculated?**
A: Reputation is based on the quality and accuracy of your contributions, including how the community votes on your facts and comments.

**Q: Can I see who voted on my content?**
A: No, voting is anonymous to prevent bias and encourage honest evaluation.

**Q: Why can't I vote on some content?**
A: You cannot vote on your own content. You also cannot vote if your account has voting restrictions due to policy violations.

**Q: How do I know if a fact is reliable?**
A: Look at the vote ratio, check the sources provided, read the discussion comments, and consider the reputation of the submitter.

### Moderation and Reporting

**Q: How long does it take to review reports?**
A: Most reports are reviewed within 24-48 hours. Complex cases may take longer.

**Q: What happens to reported content?**
A: Depending on the violation, content may be removed, edited, or left unchanged. Users may receive warnings or restrictions.

**Q: Can I appeal a moderation decision?**
A: Yes, you can appeal by contacting support with your reasoning and any additional evidence.

**Q: How do I become a moderator?**
A: Moderators are selected from active community members with high reputation scores and consistent adherence to guidelines. Applications are reviewed periodically.

### Technical Issues

**Q: Which browsers are supported?**
A: Fact Checker works best on modern browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. Internet Explorer is not supported.

**Q: Is there a mobile app?**
A: Currently, Fact Checker is available as a responsive web application that works on mobile devices. A dedicated mobile app may be developed in the future.

**Q: Why is the site running slowly?**
A: Slow performance can be due to high traffic, your internet connection, or browser issues. Try refreshing the page or clearing your browser cache.

**Q: How do I report a technical bug?**
A: Email technical issues to support@factchecker.com with details about what you were doing when the problem occurred, your browser and operating system, and any error messages.

### Privacy and Security

**Q: How is my personal information protected?**
A: We use industry-standard security measures to protect your data. See our Privacy Policy for detailed information about data collection and use.

**Q: Can other users see my email address?**
A: No, your email address is private by default. You can choose to make it visible in your privacy settings if desired.

**Q: How do I report a security concern?**
A: Email security issues to security@factchecker.com. We take security reports seriously and investigate them promptly.
